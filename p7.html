Project #7 Autobiographical Game

Proposal

| Game Description |

For my autobiographical game, I want to allow others to understand how I (as a woman) feel unsafe in everyday life— that is, when I am walking on the road alone at a certain time/place. I think everyone has this kind of experience: when you are walking home from the subway station at night, you see a shadow of a person appearing from a distance, you will change to another sidewalk immediately and risk crossing through the middle of the road. People would rather risk being hit by a car on the road—because it feels "safer" I think this is a very absurd experience but happens in my everyday life, all my female friends have this experience: It is safer to walk in the middle of the road than to pass by a stranger. I want to raise everyone's attention to this matter through my game.

| Storyboards |

In the game, you will play a female, try to dodge cars and random strangers, let yourself catch Uber, and go home. In terms of game scenes, I initially chose three times/places that I thought made me dangerous: early morning, midnight, and narrow alleys. My protagonist is a young woman, and the other obstacles are men and cars and I make my scene in pixel art style in order to let the player have the feeling that real life is as strange as the game.

Design Process

First, I used the frog game as a template to make the original idea of my gray box. I change the obstacles of the car, making it move horizontally, and let the target switch among the three scenes. I experiment with the way how my character will set back to the start when they tough the car. In my final, I changed the way the obstacle moves. The obstacle, in my final, will move back and forth in the middle of the screen, which is more different from the frog game.

Reflection

For this assignment, it is my first time and attempt I had the opportunity to make a game from scratch and I think this is an advantage and also made me encounter many challenges. I have too many complicated game ideas that I want to add to my game, for example, sound effects, dialog boxes. I have to search for all available techniques and resources on the internet and add them to my existing code. I learned how to integrate different codes fundamentally, such as making a game interface, switching scenes, and ending the screen after the game has reached its goal. It seems simple, but I spent a lot of effort to merge the three scenarios into a viable game.
